## The Reckoning

At 28, I worked 70-hour weeks for months straight. I slept 5 hours a night. I drank too much coffee, ate too much takeout, and ran on adrenaline.

I felt productive. I thought I was thriving.

At 42, I got a physical. The results were a wake-up call. Elevated blood pressure. Higher resting heart rate. Unexplained weight gain. Sleep quality that had degraded without my noticing.

The doctor asked: "How's your stress level?"

I said: "Manageable."

She said: "Your cortisol levels say otherwise."

## What Stress Actually Does

Stress isn't just feeling overwhelmed. It's a physiological state with measurable effects:

- **Cardiovascular:** Elevated blood pressure, increased heart rate, higher risk of heart disease
- **Immune:** Suppressed immune function, slower healing, more frequent illness
- **Mental:** Anxiety, depression, cognitive decline over time
- **Sleep:** Cortisol at the wrong times disrupts sleep architecture
- **Weight:** Stress hormones promote fat storage, especially abdominal fat

You can feel fine and still be accumulating damage.

## What Changed

### 1. I started measuring what I couldn't see

I got a sleep tracker. A blood pressure cuff. I started paying attention to resting heart rate.

Data makes invisible problems visible. I could see the difference between a week of 8 hours of sleep and a week of 6 hours. I could see how hard it was to recover from poor sleep when I was older.

### 2. I protected sleep like a contract

Sleep moved from "whatever happens" to a non-negotiable.

Same bedtime. Same wake time. No screens for 30 minutes before bed. The goal: 7-8 hours, consistently.

The counterintuitive finding: I got more done when I slept more. The work was better. The decisions were sharper.

### 3. I learned to notice the warning signs

Before the physical, I had normalized symptoms I shouldn't have:
- Racing thoughts at 2am
- Jaw clenching during the day
- irritability I attributed to being "busy"
- Sunday night dread

These were signals, not just features of modern life.

### 4. I built recovery into the week

Not vacation — recovery. Daily:
- 20 minutes of walking
- No meetings some mornings (deep work)
- Weekend screens check

Weekly:
- One full day off
- Physical activity I enjoy

## What I'd Tell You

Stress won't kill you today. It will kill you slowly, over decades, if you don't treat it seriously.

You can't eliminate stress. You can build resilience, create recovery time, and notice when you're running hot.

The 28-year-old version of me was proud of being busy. The 42-year-old version thinks he was an idiot.

You don't have to wait until 42.
